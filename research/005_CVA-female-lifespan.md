# Track A Direction 2: CVA across female reproductive lifespan

## Primary sources

**de Jager et al. (2025)** — *Sports Medicine* (IF 13.0). Living systematic review. Already our Paper 002.
**von Holzen et al. (2016)** — *Climacteric* (IF 3.0). Review: 45 studies on estrogen + HRV.

## Key finding: HRV follows estrogen

The evidence is consistent across 45+ studies: **HRV tracks ovarian estrogen production.** When estrogen rises → HRV rises (more vagal control). When estrogen drops → HRV drops (sympathetic dominance).

## The female lifespan HRV curve

| Life stage | RMSSD trend | Key mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Puberty (青春期, 10-15) | Rising → peak | Estrogen surge, developing vagal tone |
| Reproductive years (育龄期, 15-45) | Stable with cycle swings | Cycle: follicular +3 to -9% luteal |
| Perimenopause (围绝经期, 45-52) | Declining | Estrogen irregularity → vagal control weakens |
| Early postmenopause (<5yr, 绝经早期) | Sharp drop | pNN50: 4.3→1.6 (-63%) |
| Late postmenopause (>5yr, 绝经晚期) | Stabilized low | pNN50: 1.6→2.4 (slight recovery?) |
| 70+ years | Progressive decline | Combined aging + low estrogen |

## Specific numeric data (from de Jager 2025)

- **pNN50**: Premenopause 4.3±6.2 → Early postmenopause 1.6±1.7 → Late postmenopause 2.4±2.5 (p=0.001)
- **Middle-aged (42-56) vs young (18-35)**: RMSSD significantly lower (p<0.01)
- **LF power**: Early postmenopause 0.04±0.02 and late postmenopause 0.03±0.01, both higher than premenopause 0.02±0.01 (p=0.001) — sympathetic dominance increases
- **HF power**: Early postmenopause higher (0.09±0.09, p=0.042) — likely a compensatory or methodological artifact

## Estrogen mechanism (from von Holzen 2016)

- Estrogen (雌激素) directly supports vagal (迷走神经) activity
- All 45 studies agreed: HRV declines after menopause
- Oral contraceptives (口服避孕药): **do NOT significantly alter vagal modulation** — but our Paper 002 found they do. This discrepancy is worth noting
- Estrogen-only hormone therapy (雌激素替代疗法) improves HRV
- Combined estrogen+progestogen therapy (雌孕激素联合) **reverses** the estrogen benefit — progestogen may be detrimental to vagal tone

## Conservative oral contraceptive users

This is directly relevant to Hidden Chain users:
- Paper 002 (de Jager): CHC users have lower RMSSD (-0.51ms vs +4.65ms in natural cyclers, p<0.001)
- von Holzen 2016: no significant change — but this was measured in the 2000s with different devices

**Recommendation**: Trust de Jager. CHC users should have a separate calibration curve.

1. **Three calibration curves needed**: (a) natural menstrual, (b) CHC user, (c) postmenopausal
