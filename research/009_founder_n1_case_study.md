# Paper N-of-1: Founder Self-Validation

**Title (working)**: Volitional Vagal Recruitment in a Long-Term Zen Practitioner with Anxiety Disorder History: A Single-Subject Case Study Using Consumer Wearable HRV

**Subject**: Xue Ning, 34F, severe generalized anxiety disorder (GAD) in early 20s, recovered via 10 years of Zen/meditation practice (current daily practice: ~1 hour silent sitting).

**Device**: Huawei Watch (PPG-based IBI extraction)

**Date range**: 2026 (ongoing)

---

## Thayer's Prediction (2009, NVI model)

The Neurovisceral Integration Model predicts that the Central Autonomic Network (CAN) — comprising medial prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate, insula, amygdala, and periaqueductal gray — has **trainable plasticity**. Through repeated practice, the prefrontal cortex can develop stronger inhibitory control over the amygdala, manifesting as:

1. Higher baseline parasympathetic tone (RMSSD)
2. Faster vagal reactivation after stress
3. Possibly, **volitional recruitment of vagal tone** — the ability to consciously enter a parasympathetic-dominant state

## This Subject's Data (n=1)

| Condition | RMSSD (ms) | Implied Autonomic Age |  |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anxiety episode (no breathing) | 25-28 | 50+ |  |
| Resting baseline (no training) | 43 | 36 |  |
| 1 min slow breathing | 48 | 30 |  |
| 1 hour Zen meditation | 90-120 | <20 | deep-sleep level |

**Recovery dynamics observed**: HRV returns to baseline within seconds when breathing stops, indicating the state is volitionally maintained, not tonically elevated.

## Control Subject (Same Age, No Training)

| Condition | RMSSD (ms) |
|---|---|
| Resting | 48 |
| Stable daily range | 45-55 |
| Deep sleep (occasional peak) | 90-100 |

The control cannot reach 90-120 outside of deep sleep. Cannot raise HRV 20 points in 1 minute via breathing.

## Implications

**This subject has developed a capability that the control has not:** the ability to volitionally recruit vagal tone beyond the typical ceiling. This is consistent with NVI's prediction of CAN plasticity through meditation training, but provides a unique single-subject demonstration of the upper bound achievable through long-term practice.

**Algorithm accuracy check (independent validation):**
- Algorithm output: HRV 43 → Autonomic Age 36
- Subjective self-assessment: agrees (subjectively feels "average middle-aged adult, not elderly, not teen")
- Independent check on control (HRV ~48): Algorithm output Autonomic Age 32-37 → consistent with control's actual age
- **Both algorithm outputs match subjective and objective baselines** → algorithm reliable for non-extreme range

## Open Questions (Future Research)

1. Can the resting baseline of 43 be lifted closer to 90 through longer or different training?
2. Is the 28→48 jump the same neural pathway as the 30→120 jump, or are they different mechanisms?
3. Does this subject's post-meditation HRV ceiling (120) represent the maximum achievable for any human, or is it individual?
4. Why does the control subject's resting HRV sit at 48 (already above subject's 43) without any training? Is this genetic ceiling vs. environmental damage?
