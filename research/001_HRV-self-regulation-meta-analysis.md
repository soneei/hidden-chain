# Paper 001: HRV as a biomarker for self-regulation

**Title**: Heart rate variability indices as bio-markers of top-down self-regulatory mechanisms: A meta-analytic review
**Authors**: Jacob B. Holzman, David J. Bridgett
**Journal**: Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews (IF ~9.0)
**Year**: 2017 | **DOI**: 10.1016/j.neubiorev.2016.12.032

## Why this paper matters

**The definitive meta-analysis.** 123 studies, N=14,347 participants. This is the paper you cite when someone asks "is HRV really a valid biomarker for emotion regulation (情绪调节)?" It's published in one of the top journals in neuroscience, far stronger than the narrative reviews often cited in our field.

## Key findings

| Metric | Value | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Overall effect | r = 0.09, p < 0.001 | Small but robust positive correlation |
| 95% CI | [0.07, 0.12] | Narrow — high precision |
| Fail-Safe N | 5,334 (比率: 62.75) | Extremely resistant to publication bias (发表偏倚) |
| Age moderation | Stronger in older samples | HRV → self-regulation link grows with age |

## Moderator analysis (what does NOT change the result)

All tested moderators showed **negligible differences** — meaning the HRV → self-regulation link is consistent across:
- Different types of self-regulation (executive function, emotion regulation, effortful control)
- Measurement methods (behavioral tasks, questionnaires)
- HRV computation techniques (RMSSD, HF, SDNN)
- At-risk vs healthy samples
- Resting vs task-phase measurement

This is actually excellent news for Hidden Chain — it means our signal is robust across measurement approaches.

## Relevance to Hidden Chain

- Confirms HRV as a **valid, defensible biomarker** for self-regulation capacity
- Effect is small but real — this is why we need the multi-layered approach (cycle calibration + recovery + TCM) rather than looking at raw HRV alone
- The large Fail-Safe N (5,334) means this finding is extremely unlikely to be overturned by future research
